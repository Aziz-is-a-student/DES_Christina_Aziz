The F-Function is the heart of each Feistel round in DES. It takes a 32-bit half-block and a 48-bit subkey, then produces a new 32-bit output by mixing, substituting, and permuting, injecting both confusion and diffusion.

  • Expansion (E-Box):
    Expand the 32-bit input to 48 bits by duplicating and permuting certain bits so that each S-box receives overlapping data.
  • Key Mixing (XOR):
    XOR the expanded 48-bit data with the 48-bit round subkey, introducing key-dependent variation.
  • Substitution (S-Boxes):
    Split into eight 6-bit blocks; each passes through its own S-box, substituting 6 bits for 4 bits to add non-linearity.
  • Permutation (P-Box):
    Permute the combined 32-bit S-box output according to a fixed table, spreading bits across positions for diffusion.

Finally, this 32-bit result is XORed with the other half-block in the Feistel structure, completing one DES round.
