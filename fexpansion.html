What is the Expansion (E) P-Box?

The Expansion P-Box is an operation to expand the right 32-bit half of a block to 48 bits.


The E-table expands 32 bits into 48 bits by duplicating and permuting selected bits.


In particular, bit #1 (the leftmost bit) ends up both at the start of the first 6-bit subgroup and at the end of the last subgroup.


As a result, each of the 8 overlapping 6-bit groups ‚Äúgrabs‚Äù two boundary bits from adjacent 4-bit blocks, creating an overlap that strengthens diffusion.
